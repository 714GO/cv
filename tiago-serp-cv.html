Can Tiago turn complex insight into distinctive content?
At the ECB, the insight was uncomfortable: EU youth were the least likely to encounter institutional financial content through any channel the ECB used — yet they were making their first financial decisions in the dark.

The concept was EuroSteps — a campaign structured not around institutional messaging but around real financial milestones: first job, first apartment, first savings decision. Each piece was built around an actual search query, not a communications priority. GenAI was used to accelerate research clustering and first-draft structure — every output was then edited heavily and fact-checked against primary ECB sources before publication. The campaign outperformed institutional baseline on both engagement and search visibility.

Has Tiago built a scalable content operating model?
At the ECB, three institutional websites were operating as separate content operations — inconsistent standards, conflicting content, no shared editorial view. The case for change was simple: if three domains are cited together in an AI-generated response about European financial regulation, inconsistency between them erodes the authority of all three.

I built a shared editorial governance framework defining what each team could publish independently and what required cross-domain sign-off. I then embedded shared SEO standards, tone of voice guidelines, and structured data requirements into Jira workflows — governance built into production rather than bolted on at the end. Automated reporting via Microsoft Power Automate reduced monitoring overhead by 30%. The result was a unified publishing rhythm, measurably improved cross-domain consistency, and a single analytics view for the first time across three institutional domains.
